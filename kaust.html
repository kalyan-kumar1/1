Model architecture

A fully convolutional pipeline, end to end.

Rather than treating each pedestrian as an isolated sequence, the network keeps the scene as a spatial tensor throughout — track history, social context, and prediction all live on the same grid, which is what lets the model reason about crowd interaction without an explicit graph.

01
Frame ingest
Rolling window of video frames is decoded and pedestrians are localized and assigned track IDs.
02
Occupancy encoding
Track positions and velocities are rasterized into a multi-channel spatial grid over the scene.
03
FCN feature extraction
Stacked convolutions with dilation extract social and spatial features while preserving grid geometry.
04
Trajectory decoder
Transposed convolutions regress multi-modal displacement fields for each tracked agent.
05
Intention head
A lightweight classifier reads pooled features per agent to output a short-horizon intention label.

Intention tracking

Reading motion cues before they resolve into a path.

Displacement alone is ambiguous — a slowing pedestrian could be stopping, turning, or yielding. The intention head folds heading change, deceleration, and proximity to scene landmarks (curbs, crossings, doorways) into a single short-horizon label.

Crossing
Steady heading toward a crossing region with sustained forward velocity.
Waiting
Near-zero velocity sustained across multiple frames at a curb or junction.
Walking through
Consistent heading and speed with no landmark proximity — transit, not intent to stop.
Loitering
Low net displacement with high heading variance over an extended window.
